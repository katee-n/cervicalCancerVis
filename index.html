Cervical Cancer Visualizations

Scrollytelling of Cervical Cancer Risk Factors. Filterable by age.

The dataset “Cervical Cancer VS Demographic, Habits, MedHistory” contains information about 858 different patients diagnosed with cervical cancer and factors that may have caused this disease. The dataset was collected at 'Hospital Universitario de Caracas' in Caracas, Venezuela and should emphasize the necessity of preventive measures regarding cervical cancer.

We decided to use this dataset because cervical cancer isn’t discussed as much as we think it should. It affects lots of people, although it could be prevented or diagnosed earlier more often. Even in Austria, especially young people, aren’t informed as much about this disease and its causes, preventions and symptoms.

Riskfactors

The wordcloud illustrates the major risk factors associated with cervical cancer - smoking, IUDs, STIs, birth control pills, and multiple sexual partners.

The more prominent - or rather bigger - a word is, the greater its impact as a risk factor for cervical cancer.
This is due to the fact that more participants - who were diagnosed with cervical cancer - are or were exposed to the risk factor.

Through hovering above one of the words a tooltip appears with the exact number of people.

Checkups done

These two circles show the amount of people who did do checkups vs the people who didn't do checkups. As these exams can help with detecting cervical cancer quite early, we think it is important to emphasize the contrast in size between these two groups.

Can you guess which bubble represents which group of people?

Hover over them to find out if you are right!

Checkups distribution

The donut chart is an extension of the one pie chart above, which represents the number of examinations performed before the participants got their diagnosis.
The donut chart is intended to give deeper insight into which of the four examinations was done how often.

An examination for cervical cancer includes cytological screening, biopsy and colposcopy. The latter consists of four stages, the last two of which are the Hinselmann and Schiller tests.
All four check-up types are represented here.

Through hovering above one of the sections in the donut chart a tooltip will appear that shows the exact number of people who did the certain examination.
